---
title: "Staffing Tab"
---

The Staffing tab manages the staff members connected to an excursion. Use it to add staff, assign excursion roles, record attendance, and check whether required responsibilities are covered.

## What You Can See

- A Staff members heading with a staff count.
- A description of the staffing list.
- A read-only note if you do not have permission to make changes.
- Warnings if required responsible roles are missing.
- Alerts if staff roles cannot be loaded or no active roles are available.
- An Add staff action when editing is allowed.
- A staff grid with search, filters, editable fields, and row actions.

## Main Actions

### Add Staff

Use Add staff to open a modal where you can add one or more staff members. Each row lets you choose a staff member and assign an excursion role. You can add another row before saving if multiple people need to be added at once.

### Assign or Change Roles

Use the Role field in the grid to assign each staff member's excursion role. Roles may use icons or labels to make responsibilities easier to scan.

### Mark Attendance

Use the Attending excursion checkbox to indicate whether a staff member will attend the excursion.

### Add Notes

Use the Notes field for staffing details, such as responsibilities, limitations, or planning context.

### Remove Staff

Use the row action menu to remove a staff member from the excursion when editing is allowed.

## Staff Grid

The grid can include:

- Staff member, including pending invite indicators when relevant.
- Role, for supervision or excursion responsibilities.
- Attending excursion, for attendance tracking.
- Notes, for extra context.
- Actions, such as removing the staff member.

The toolbar lets you search and filter by staff, role, attendance, and notes.

## Common Workflows

### Build the supervision team

Add staff members, assign roles, and mark who will attend the excursion.

### Resolve missing responsible roles

Review any missing role alert, then add staff or update roles until required responsibilities are covered.

### Review pending invitations

Check pending invite indicators and send reminders where available and appropriate.

## Tips

- Confirm staffing before submitting the excursion for review.
- Keep role assignments clear so reviewers understand who is responsible for each area.
- Use notes for practical context that does not fit into the role name.
